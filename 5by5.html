Five IT Events

Invention of the Printing Press

The printing press was invented around 1440s. The printing press is an important invention as it meant information could be spread quicker and accurately. Being able to copy information quicker, largely contributed to the increase of reading literate for the public

Invention of the Telephone

The first telephone was Invented in 1876, significantly changing the way people communicate. During this time telegrams was the main source of long-distance communication which took days for a message to be received, however the telephone proved to be much more efficient, which continued and will continue to develop.

Invention of the computer

The first computer was invented in 1832, however the first computer wasn’t built until 1940s. the invention of the computer is important as it is used globally, and it is an important tool in daily life as it can perform many tasks in a short amount of time

Release of the World Wide Web

On April 30, 1993, CERN realised the World Wide Web to the public. This was a significant event as this was the first open licenced software made available for all globally to use. The World Wide Web allows many to share, retrieve documents, information and communicate for basically anywhere.

Invention of the cellphone

The first handheld mobile phone was made in 1973 and released to the public in 1983. The invention of the cell phone is important as it didn’t require cords, so it portable. Now day’s phones are used for more then calling and they are an essential part of daily activities.

People in IT

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer, born in 1815. Ada Lovelace is an important figure within the IT industry as she was considered the first computer programmer. Lovelace realized that the computer could follow a series of simple instructions, to create program, to perform a complex calculation.

Mary Allen Wilkes

Mary Allen Wilkes is a former computer programmer and hardware engineer. She designed the console and the first operating system for LINC and continued to design many operating systems. She is considered to be the first computer home user after she designed and used a computer.

Joy Buolamwini

Joy Buolamwini is a computer scientist and digital activist who founded the Algorithmic Justice League. She is an important figure in the IT Industry as she explores the large racial and gender bias in AI services. She works to create more ethnical and inclusive technology.

Sister Mary Kenneth Keller

Sister Mary Kenneth Keller was an American educator and pioneer in computer science. She was the first Women in the United States to earn a Ph.D. in computer science and helped develop BASIC, a programming language. Additionally, she advocated for the involvement of women in tech.

Peter Beck

Peter Beck is a New Zealand entrepreneur and founder of Rocket Lab, an aerospace manufacturer. He is known for leading the small satellite launch revolution, Peter also played a role in establishing international treaties and legislation to enable orbital launch capability from New Zealand.

A BCI is a computer base system that translates brain signals into commands that is then outputted. As this technology improves in the future it can be used by many in NZ as an assist for ones with physical disabilities to move limbs they weren’t before able too

Types of IT Roles

Software Devaloper

Software developers create and maintain programs and applications. They may do things such as research and create pieces of software working with a client In New Zealand the Job opportunity is good with the salary range being between $50k - $200k per year.

User Experience designer

A user experience designer is focused on making sure an application or website meets the requirements or needs of the user. They look at many aspects of the development including design, usability, function, and marketing. In NZ the job opportunity is good with the salary range between $90k - $150k.

Test Analyst

A test analyst is responsible for testing to ensure software or hardware is working as intended. They do this by designing, developing and administering a series of tests and evaluations that identify any potential issues. The job opportunity is good with salary range between $80k - $200k.

Game Developer

A game developer is someone who works to create games. They take an idea and code it into a playable game, with a plot, characters, visuals and sound. The job opportunity in NZ is poor with a salary range between $44k - $80k depending on experience.

Network Administrator

A network administrator is someone who sets up computer networks including the hardware. They also maintain and upgrade existing computer networks, fixing networking, hardware and communication problems when arise. The job opportunity is good, and the salary range is between $80k - $140k.
